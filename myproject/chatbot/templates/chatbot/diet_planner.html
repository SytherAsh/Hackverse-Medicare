Dietary Advice Based on CBC Haemogram Results

  • CBC results are limited: While useful, they don't provide a full picture of your nutritional status or overall health.
  • Individualized needs: Dietary recommendations depend on age, sex, activity level, health goals, and medical conditions.

What the CBC Results Suggest

  • RBC count: Slightly above the normal range.
  • RDW (Red Cell Distribution Width): Elevated, indicating variation in red blood cell size.

These findings could indicate:

  • Iron deficiency: Elevated RDW may suggest iron deficiency, even if haemoglobin levels are normal.
  • Other conditions: Potential underlying issues affecting blood cell production.

What You Should Do

  • Consult a doctor: Discuss the results with your doctor, who may order further tests to investigate the cause.
  • Seek a registered dietitian: A dietitian can create a personalized meal plan based on your individual needs.

General Healthy Eating Tips

  • Fruits and vegetables: Aim for at least five servings per day.
  • Whole grains: Choose brown rice, quinoa, whole-wheat bread, and oatmeal.
  • Lean proteins: Include fish, poultry, beans, lentils, and tofu.
  • Healthy fats: Use olive oil, avocados, and nuts, and avoid saturated and trans fats.
  • Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water throughout the day.

Important Notes: While the findings from your CBC haemogram are helpful, they are only part of the overall health picture. Always consult healthcare professionals for personalized advice and follow-up tests if necessary.
